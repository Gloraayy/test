Nelson Mandela

The unforgettable African hero

The great revolutionarist that fought against Apartheid

This is the brief history of Nelson Mandela

  • 1918 - He was born in the Eastern Cape in South Africa.
  • 1943 - He joined the African National Congress (ANC) as an activist.
  • 1944 - He married Walter Sisulu’s cousin, Evelyn Mase.
  • 1948 - Mandela and others in the ANC began advocating direct action against apartheid.
  • 1952 - He co-founded the first black legal partnership in South Africa
  • 1958 - He and his wife divorced.
  • 1962 - Mandela secretly left South Africa using the adopted name David Motsamayi.
  • 1964 - Mandela spent 27 years of his life in prison from 1964 till 1990.
  • 1993 - Nelson Mandela was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1994 - Nelson Mandela was the first black President of South Africa till 1999.
  • 1998 - On his 80th birthday in he married Graça Machel, his third wife.
  • 1999 - He retired from politics but continued to take part in activism and philanthropy.
  • 2013 - He died on December 5th at the age of 95 at his home in Johannesburg.


"I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die."

Mandela'speech to the court when facing the death penalty on the 20th of April 1964
